Hypothesis
Best under shift
Predictive SSL + adaptation

Our main contribution. I-JEPA predicts abstract representations of masked regions rather than reconstructing pixels or matching augmented views. We hypothesize this produces more transferable features under domain shift. Combined with entropy-based LayerNorm adaptation at test time — requiring zero labels and updating only 36,864 of 86M parameters — this is the proposed solution to the catastrophic degradation observed across all three baselines.

03Full Baseline Comparison

Key finding: Three different pretraining paradigms — supervised, contrastive SSL (DINO), and reconstructive SSL (MAE) — all fail catastrophically under domain shift. DINO is 10.90% more accurate on the source domain but retains only 7.0% under shift. MAE collapses to 0.06% on BloodMNIST — essentially zero. Better in-domain accuracy does not equal robustness.

04Key Observations
All three baselines fail catastrophically
Supervised, contrastive SSL, and reconstructive SSL all collapse under domain shift regardless of source domain accuracy. No existing pretraining paradigm solves this.
MAE collapses to 0.06% on BloodMNIST
Pixel reconstruction pretraining produces features highly sensitive to low-level visual statistics. When imaging modality changes from tissue to blood cells, the features collapse entirely.
Better source accuracy ≠ better robustness
DINO is 10.90% more accurate than supervised on source but retains only 7.0% under shift — nearly identical to supervised at 6.6%. Source performance does not predict shift robustness.
ECE collapses under shift for all methods
Source ECE ranges 0.011–0.088. Under shift all methods become severely overconfident. Calibration failure is as dangerous as accuracy failure in clinical deployment.
Three distinct failure modes
Supervised: moderate collapse. DINO: same collapse despite better features. MAE: catastrophic collapse on some domains. Each pretraining paradigm fails differently — motivating a fundamentally different approach.
Highly reproducible results
MAE std of 0.03% is the smallest seen. All methods show std below 0.37% confirming 3-seed protocol is sufficient for reliable conclusions.
